About

The world's money.
Made visible.

WhoIsPaying.com was built on a single conviction: that the most important decisions in the world — wars, elections, trade routes, sanctions — are shaped not by ideology, but by money. And that money deserves to be seen.

Editorial Policy

WhoIsPaying.com is a financial accountability platform, not a political one. We cover war financing, lobbying, and geopolitical money flows as matters of public financial interest — the same way Bloomberg, Reuters, or the Financial Times would.

We do not take sides on any conflict. We do not glorify, condone, or dismiss any war or political position. Every claim is labelled with a data integrity tag and sourced to a named public record.

Our editorial standard: follow the money, not the ideology.

Our Vision

We believe financial transparency is a public good. When a government funds a war, when a corporation lobbies a port project, when a foreign state registers agents in Washington — that information exists in public records. It's just fragmented, technical, and deliberately hard to navigate.

WhoIsPaying changes that. We combine AI-powered analysis with verifiable public data to make complex financial influence legible to anyone — journalists, researchers, investors, students, and citizens.

Our goal is not to take sides. It is to follow the money — wherever it leads.

Data Integrity Promise

Every answer on WhoIsPaying carries one of three labels — not as decoration, but as a commitment:

VERIFIEDConfirmed in a named public record — FEC filing, FARA registration, official budget document, or peer-reviewed dataset.
ESTIMATEDDerived from modelling, aggregation, or credible secondary reporting. We show our methodology where possible.
UNKNOWNDark money, shell entities, or unverifiable flows. We name the gap — not pretend it isn't there.
